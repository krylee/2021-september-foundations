Bio

I live in Omaha with my daughter, Lily, and my dog, Minnie. I love to read, re-watch episodes of Scrubs and The Simpsons, attend concerts, and try new restaurants. Before becoming a teacher, I worked for many years as a claims examiner. I began teaching for Omaha Public Schools in 2017 and have been teaching for almost 5 years.

Education

Before deciding to become an educator, I attended Metropolitan Community College, where I earned an Associate of Arts degree in Liberal arts. I then transferred to Creighton University where I earned a Bachelor of Arts in English. Just a few months after finishing my bachelor's degree, I decided I wanted to teach, so I attended College of Saint Mary, where I earned a Master of Arts in Teaching. Most recently, I earned a Master of Science in Special Education at the University of Nebraska Omaha.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that all students should have equitable learning opportunities and a safe learning environment. As an educator, I work to meet students' unique learning needs and provide them with the tools they need to be successful students. Relationships come first, and I believe that students learn best when they trust their teacher. Finally, I believe that partnering with families helps foster students' learning and provides them with a strong support system as they navigate their education.
